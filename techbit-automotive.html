Self-Driving Cars

Society of Autonomous Vehicles identifies 6 levels:

  • level-0: Driver controls the vehicle. The steering, the throttle, break and all other controls of the vehicle.
  • level-1: Driver Assistance Level. Driver must be fully engaged. Vehicle supports driver for either Steering or Acceleration. e.g. cruise control.
  • level-2: Partial Automation. Vehicle does few functions automatically, but driver still must be engaged. e.g. Automatic Cruise Control, Automatic Lane Keeping
  • level-3: Conditional Automation. Vehicle drives autonomously, but Driver MUST take over whenever necessary.
  • level-4: High Automation - No human interference. May not have steering wheel, break, throttle. Vehicle controls all functions autonomously but within certain Geofence.
  • level-5: Full Automation. Anywhere and everywhere in drivable area.

ROS (Robotic Operating System)

https://www.ros.org/ is not a operating system, but actually is a middleware. It is a collection of many libraries, tools. It enables collaborative style of software development. They say ROS = Plumbing + Tools + Capabilities + Ecosystem.

By design ROS is distributed and moduler.

ROS comprises of majorly 3 parts:

(1) Communication infrastructure
- Publish/Scribe anonymous mesage passing
- Recording and playback of messages
- Remote Procedure Calls
- Distributed Parameter System
(2) Robot-specific Features
- Standard Message Definitions for Robots
- Robot Geometry Library
- Robot Description Language
- Preemptable Remote Procedure Calls
- Diagnostics
- Pose Estimation
- Localization
- Mapping
- Navigation

Autosar

AUTomotive Open System ARchitecture (AUTOSAR) is developed by automobile manufacturers and vendors, suppliers, service providers and companies from automotive electronics, semiconductor and software together in order to standardize the development of vehicle software. Before AUTOSAR every manufacturer and vendors used to create their own version of software and its features, interfaces were not compatible with each other. With standardization of the architecture and the interfaces each vendor, manufacturer can develop, integrate the sub-systems, and build on top of each others works without much thinking of compatibility. Modifications, upgrade and update becomes easy. It targets increased reuse and exchangibility of SW modules between OEMs and suppliers.

Before AUTOSAR, traditional approach,
[- Application Software -]
[------- Software -------]
[------- Hardware -------]

After AUTOSAR
[--- Application Software --]
[- Standardized Middleware -]
[-- Standardized Basic SW --]
[---- Hardware Specific ----]
[-------- Hardware ---------]

Thus benefits,

  • HW and SW - widely independent of each other
  • Development can be decoupled, so reduced time and costs
  • Reuse of SW, enhanced quality and efficiency

ASIL - Automotive Safety Integrity Level
Risk Classification System defined by ISO 26262 for functional safety
  1. Depending on (i) Severity (ii) Exposure (iii) Controllability
  2. 4 levels are defined A, B, C and D
  3. A is lowest
  4. D is Highest
  5. For instance
  6. - Rear Lights is ASIL-A
  7. - Break Lights is ASIL-B
  8. - Headlights is ASIL-B
  9. - Engine Management is ASIL-C to ASIL-D
  10. - AirBag is ASIL-D
  11. - Electric Power Steering is ASIL-D
It helps in (i) establishing safety measurements to migrate risks to acceptable levels. (ii) Managing, Tracking safety requirements (iii) Ensures standardized procedures are followed
From Service Interface Definition, two parts are created (i) Service Proxy (ii) Service Skeleton. Proxy is used by the Client Application and Skeleton is used by Service Application.

Functional Safety (FUSA)

Motivation

accidents occure, injuries to lives of people, damage to physical systems, harm to nature, loss of business

Cause, relying on a system that is possibly not safe-system

Safety

Risks are always there. but Safety is Absence of unreasonable risk.

Vocabulary

Harm: injury, damage

Hazard: Potential source of harm

Hazardous Event: Hazardous situation that result in harm

Risk: Probability + Severity of a potential harm (High/Medium/Low)

Safety: No unacceptable risk.

Safety Function: Function to keep 'safe state'

Safe State: operating mode without unreasonable level of risk

Safety Integrity: Integrity level that safety function would perform

Reliability Vs Safety

Reliable: Doesn't fail in certain timespan, May lead to hazard if fails

Safe System: May fail in certain timespan, but still be in safe state. Do NOT result in (1) Injury to inside and out outside persons (2) Least damage

Active Safety Vs Passive Safety

Active Safety: Continuously checks for safety

Passive Safety: Waits for external trigger to check safety

Security Vs Safety

Secure need not be safe.

Secure will give protection against attack but cannot guarantee Safety. e.g. If accident cause breathing impossible, then even if it is Secure from attack, it is NOT safe.

Faults

Random: Mainly due to Hardware failure

Systematic: Mainly, Design flaws, Can be found during verification stage

Common: Two or more systems fail because of 1 fault.

Safety System

Typically: 'fault' --> 'Failure' --> 'Hazard'

Safe: 'Find fault before failure', 'If failure occurs, before hazard achieve safe state
