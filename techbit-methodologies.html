History Of Agile

  • In 2001, 17 signatories gathered and they laid down the foundation of Agile Principles and Manifesto.
  • 4 Manifesto
  • 12 Agile Principals
  • But in 1930, Walter Shewhart at Bell-labs started Plan-Do-Check-Act
  • Walter's mentee W. Edwards Deming who used the knowledge in WWII
  • Toyota hired Deming
  • In 1986 Ikujiro Nonaka published "The New New Product Development Game"
  • All of this formed basis for Jeff Sutherland and Ken Sschwaber who proposed Scrum framework in 2009.

Agile Manifesto

  • Individuals and interactions -over- Processes and Tools
  • Working Product -over- Comprehensive Documentation
  • Customer Collaboration -over- Contract Negotiation
  • Responding to change -over- Following a plan

Agile is a Mindset

Described by 4 Values

Defined by 12 principles

Manifested through unlimited number of practices

Empirical Vs Defined Process

  • Empirical - For Complex Projects, due to (People, Process, Technology), working in fact-based, experienced-based, Evidence-based manner
  • 3 Pillars of Empirical Process - Transparency, Inspection, Adaptation

Scrum Framework

  • Iterative, Incremental Development
  • Right time
  • Value
  • Self-organizing team
  • Higher Productivity, Faster
  • Customer priorities are evaluated
  • Feedback to team
  • Starts with vage Vision
  • Owner presents prioritized list (Product Backlog)
  • Sprint is timebox (<1 month)

Roles and Events

  • 3 Roles:
    • Product Owner
      • Represents Stakeholders
      • Requirements
      • Interested in ROI
      • Prioritizes product backlog
      • Most valuable functinality is implemented first
      • Provides feedback
    • Team
      • Is cross functional
      • self sufficient
      • self organized
      • skilled
      • commited and available
      • is accountable
    • Scrum Master
      • Scrum process
      • Makes sure everybody plays by rules
      • Conducts ceremonies
      • Removes impediments for team
  • 5 Events: 
    • Sprint Planning
      • Start of sprint
      • Inputs:
        • Product Backlog
        • Latest Increment
        • Velocity of Dev Team
      • Planning
        • Which PBIs to include in sprint
        • How will we do it?
        • Team needs to raise issues
      • Outputs
        • Sprint Goals
        • Sprint Backlog

      • Daily Scrum

      • Timeboxed
      • Full team presence
      • Focus on (What did yesterday? What will do today? Any impediments)

      Sprint Review

      • At end of each sprint
      • To check the increment
      • What functionalities "Done"
      • Update product backlog

      Sprint Retrospective

      • What went well?
      • What could be improved?
      • Dev Team, Scrum Master, Product Owner take part

      Sprint

      • Is an event that includes other 4 events
      • Once one sprint ends other starts

    • Scrum Artifacts

    • Product Backlog
      • Prioritized
      • Includes everything from requirements
      • Constantly evolving
    • Increment
      • Usable Addition
      • New update of the product which owner may choose to release
    • Sprint backlog
      • All committed user stories, tasks
      • All needs to be 'developed', 'integrated', 'documented' and 'tested'

Product Backlog Refinement

  • is an act of adding details, estimate and order to Product Backlog
  • Helps Identify Value in an item
  • Helps Eliminate waste
  • Ongoing process, collaborate with Team and Stakeholders
  • Items are reviewed and revised
  • It is NOT an Event
  • PO can do it as its discretion
  • Helps set Product Vision, Roadmap and Acceptance Criteria

Estimation Techniques

  • Guessing, approximate, Usable Value
  • Decision Making
  • Helps in building strong team coordination
  • Determining deadlines
  • Relative Estimation (Comparing) [Small, Medium, Large, Extra Large]
  • Planning Poker
    • Fibonacci sequence or similar
    • Estimators completes discussion
    • Each Privately then reveal the Value
    • Then High/Low estimators tell their reasons
    • Then repeat estimation
    • Once all conviced a value is finalized
  • T-Shirt size:
    • XS (1)
    • S (3)
    • M (5-8)
    • L (13-21)
    • XL (30+)
    • Sizes can vary from team to team basis
    • Important to keep them consistant once finalized
  • Bucket Size:
    • 0, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 20, 30, 50, 100, 200
    • First item acts as reference for rest of the items
    • And then put into relevant buckets
    • Its fast, collaborative, provides relative results and encourages accountability

Scrum Master

  • Servant Leader
  • Interface between anyone from outside Scrum team for the team
  • Helps everyone, to maximize the value
  • Responsible for team to follow Agile Values
  • Clearing Obstacles
  • Establishing environment for team to be effective
  • Addressing Team Dynamics
  • Ensuring Good Relationships (within and outside) Scrum Team
  • Protecting team from outiside distractions/interruptions
